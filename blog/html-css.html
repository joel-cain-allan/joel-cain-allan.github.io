Margins, Borders and Paddings

What are the differences? Where does the madness end?

26 March 2024

  • Basics
  • Margin

  • Padding
  • Content

What are the basics?

The CSS box model breaks an HTML element down into four sub-sections for the sake of sizing:

These four parts of the box model each affect the way a browser allocates space for the element.


What is a margin?

The margin is the distance that should be left between the border and other elements. There's a bit of a 'gotcha' with margins however: Top and Bottom margins can collapse. A collapsing margin is not additive, and instead treats the total margin of each element as the larger of their individual margins. For example, two elements with vertical margins of 40px and 50px respectively may only have an effective distance of 50px! Left and right margins, however, are always additive. If those same elements were arranged horizontally in an inline-block the effective total margin distance would be 40+50=90px. Don't get caught out by this little quirk!


What is a border?

A border is exactly what it says on the tin: an optionally visible perimeter around the HTML element. You can control the width, style etc of this perimeter, but the important part for this discussion is the width of the border. By default, the width grows outward, meaning that the final size of an element is influenced by the size of its border. It is possible, however, to change this behaviour: setting box-sizing: content-box; will cause the browser to account for any border or padding when you set the element's width or height. Speaking of padding...


What is padding?

Padding is the space between the content and the border. It's mostly used as 'whitespace' (though it doesn't have to be white!), and can enhance the visibiity of the content within the element.


What is content?

In the web development world content isn't just text, it's the core part of any HTML element. It could be an image, a clickable button, a link, a form... anything you see on a webpage is, at its core, the content section of an HTML element wrapped in all the other parts of the box model.
