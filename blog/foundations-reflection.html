Foundations Reflections

That went quickly!

15 April 2024


What are three new things you have learnt about yourself and your ego due to the core learning?

Firstly, I learned that I'm more confident than I thought I was. I put myself out of my comfort zone by making sure to engage with the Dev Academy community, and aside from a flat joke or two it was all positive.
Secondly, I learned that I'm eager to get into the workforce. I thought I was, but spending time with some set tasks and goals has been very pleasant. Perhaps that's more from the tech side than core, but it feels valid to me.
Thirdly, I learned that I'm either I'm very bad about the core part of the curriculum, or I was already very aware of myself and my limitations prior to the beginnings of Foundations. Perhaps that sounds like a cop-out, but core focused on things I'd already thought about. I had plenty of time in the last decade for self-reflection, and looking for ways to improve lead to touching on a lot of this content. That's not a bad thing at all though - there's a degree of reassurance in knowing these have been questions that I'd already asked and answered.

What are the roles of values, empathy, and self-awareness in learning and programming

Learning is a process that takes everyone different amounts of time. Empathy is valuable both for having patience with those who learn more slowly than you, and also in being willing to spend time helping them upskill. The same will be true in the workplace - everyone are going to be at slightly different levels, and we must hope that those more knowledgeable than ourselves will be willing to pass on the value of their experience. We must be self-aware enough to know the things we can do, and those we can't do yet... and be willing to put in the work on those 'not yet' skills. That also means having the self-awareness to know when you need help, and the willingness to ask for it.

What has surprised you the most about the core learning?

Probably the biggest surprise was how much of a focus it was during Foundations. I knew it couldn't possibly be 100% tech skills time, and that Dev Academy prides itself on focusing on human development and skills rather than purely 1s and 0s, but it was still a bigger part of the curriculum than I'd expected. With hindsight I should have expected it - there are some big concepts being taught on the tech side, and there's only so much a brain can take in over a week. Wise to mix up the focus a bit every week.

What were the most challenging aspects of the core learning?

I guess the toughest part is making sure to engage with it openly, without bias or prejudice, without preconception or reservation. This week in particular I feel a little flippant with some of these answers despite them being true and accurate - it feels like there's an expectation of something profound (and that hasn't materialized!). As a very definite introvert, and the only male in my family it can be a little tricky to be an open book, but I'm doing my best!

Why do you think we, a programming school, are spending so much time focusing on core learning in a web development Bootcamp course?

Well, it's certainly not a secret that Dev Academy views these skills as important (as do employers), and from what we've been told employers have seen it as a point of difference for Dev Academy graduates vs some of the other candidates they may see. It's also valuable in preparing people for the real work in bootcamp - the more comfortable you are with your learning style, stress-response etc the better you'll be able to handle the pressure and work with others.

Does the time you spent studying core learning here feel like a waste of time? Should you have just used that time to practise programming instead? Justify your answer.

For me personally? I wouldn't say it was a waste of time, but it wasn't exceptionally useful for me either; but only because we're covering concepts I've already thought about. I'm on board with the importance of core skills along with tech skills, and participated in the core stuff happily. Frankly 'things I'm already familiar with' was the name of the game for Foundations, but that was not a surprise on the Tech side. It was a little surprising on the Core part of the curriculum, but I suppose I should have expected the possibility - not everyone who studies with Dev Academy is an old man like me!
