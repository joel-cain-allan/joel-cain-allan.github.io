The OG

What is your long term goal or career pathway?

I’m looking to move into web development, not too many frills above and beyond that. In the long term something at least semi-remote would be nice, but step one is getting certified, and step two is getting relevant industry experience. Everything beyond that can wait.


A description of your strengths and limitations when it comes to learning?

I guess we’ll start with strengths. I’m a quick study as a general rule - I have a good memory, fast-processing, and am stubborn enough to keep grinding away at something until I have it down. I always did well in school, though when I was younger I tended to breeze by rather than worrying about putting the work in. Weaknesses… I’m not terribly good at sticking to a slower pace, and if forced to I tend to get bored and disinterested with the material. Also, as I have had (or still have?) chronic fatigue, energy levels may be problematic from time to time.


What skills (non-technical core/human skills) would you like to see developed in yourself while at Dev Academy?

Cooperation and collaboration would be the big two. I’m very comfortable working on my own, and will have to develop some good habits with splitting up the work, checking in with others in a team etc. On a broader, less work-focused level I guess being slightly more extroverted wouldn’t hurt.


A commitment to how you will manage your workload in this programme. This should include: An explanation of how you will manage yourself to work productively and safely with other learners, Facilitators and industry/community representatives What you will do when things are building up and starting to get to you

Not really sure what to put for this one. I do not intend, nor ever have intended, to do anything other than work both productively and safely with others. I’m not hotheaded, or prone to lash out, or anything like that. I commit to continue with the habits of a lifetime and try to treat others as I’d want to be treated.
